# Design Strategy Note

## Direction Name

**Quiet Heat Atlas**

## Palette Choice

- Ground: `#0B0B0D`
- Panel: `#121317`
- Raised panel: `#181A1F`
- Text primary: `#F2EEE8`
- Text secondary: `#B8B2A8`
- Border: `#2A2D34`
- Accent: `#B78C56`

### Why this palette

- The black is warm enough to feel inhabited, not tech-demo cold.
- The accent bronze adds heat without becoming fake-luxury gold.
- Meetings use muted signal neutrals so conferences can carry stronger visual emphasis without adding extra accent colors.

## Typography Choice

- Serif stack: editorial and familiar, used only where authority and atmosphere matter
- Sans stack: compact and clean for controls, metadata, lists, and body
- Mono stack: tiny metadata only

### Why this typography

- The serif gives the product an authored, cultural tone.
- The sans keeps the product practical and readable once the user starts scanning.
- The mono helps small labels feel deliberate instead of decorative.

## Motion Philosophy

- Subtle reveal only.
- Motion should clarify continuity between shell, filters, and detail surfaces.
- No bounce, no novelty transitions, no ornamental animation.
- Reduced-motion mode aggressively cuts back animations.

## Layout Strategy

- The homepage is map-first and large-scale.
- Supporting content below the fold is editorial, not card-gridded.
- Meetings are a split explorer on desktop and a deliberate map/list toggle on mobile.
- Conferences are selective and hierarchical: one featured record, then the current list, then archive.

## Reference Influence

- Awwwards map collection informed the map-led hero and restraint around geography.
- Awwwards minimal and black categories informed the spacing, hierarchy, and dark-surface control.
- SiteInspire black-and-white/minimal categories reinforced the no-filler composition approach.
- Godly and Mobbin influenced the filter chip, search bar, and panel cadence decisions.
